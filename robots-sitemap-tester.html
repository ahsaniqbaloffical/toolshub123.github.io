      Best practices for robots.txt & sitemap

      Robots.txt essentials

      • Keep it simple: Only disallow areas you truly don’t want crawled
      • Include sitemap: Add a direct “Sitemap:” line with absolute URL
      • Avoid crawl-delay: Rarely needed and can slow discovery
      • Don’t block assets: CSS/JS/images are needed to render pages
      Sample robots.txt 
      Sitemap: https://example.com/sitemap.xml

User-agent: *
Disallow:

      Sitemap essentials

      • Canonical URLs: Use lowercase, absolute, 200-OK URLs
      • Keep clean: Remove redirects and non-indexable pages
      • Split large sites: Use a sitemap index with multiple child sitemaps
      • Update frequently: Regenerate when content changes
      Sample sitemap entry 
      <url>
  <loc>https://example.com/blog/guide</loc>
  <lastmod>2026-01-08</lastmod>
  <changefreq>weekly</changefreq>
  <priority>0.8</priority>
</url>

      Troubleshooting

      • “Add Sitemap directive” warning persists: Ensure the line exists exactly as “Sitemap: https://yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml”. The tool detects it case-insensitively anywhere in the file.
      • No <loc> entries found: Your sitemap may be HTML, a custom format, or empty. Make sure it’s valid XML following the sitemaps.org schema.
      • Mixed-case URLs: Normalize to lowercase for consistency and avoid duplicate crawl paths.
      • Redirects in sitemap: Replace with final 200-OK targets to speed discovery.
      • Proxy errors: If a domain blocks the proxy, try again or verify your server’s proxy configuration.

      FAQs

      Do I need both robots.txt and sitemap?

      Robots.txt helps control crawler access; sitemaps help discovery. You should have both, even for small sites.

      Where should I host my sitemap?

      At a stable public URL, usually /sitemap.xml. Add the sitemap directive in robots.txt and submit in Search Console.

      Can I have multiple sitemaps?

      Yes. Use a sitemap index file to list child sitemaps. You can also include multiple “Sitemap:” lines in robots.txt.

      Why avoid blocking CSS/JS?

      Search engines render pages. Blocking assets can lead to layout misinterpretation and ranking issues.
